  • The 90-day challenge is over, but your learning is not. The difference between a candidate who stops at Day 90 and one who continues is everything — attackers evolve, and defenders must too.
  • Shift from "learning" mode to "doing" mode. You now have enough foundation to contribute. Join a CTF team, do bug bounty hunting, contribute to open-source security tools, or help secure a local nonprofit's network.

🛡 Career Growth Mindset
  • Certifications are the next logical step: PNPT (most practical, highly respected), OSCP (gold standard for pentesting), Security+ (entry-level HR filter), or BTL1 (blue team, SOC-focused).
  • Apply to jobs consistently — 5-10 applications per week. The first role is the hardest; each interview makes you better. Treat rejections as data, not failure.
  • Keep building in public. Continue posting on LinkedIn and GitHub. The audience you built during 90 days will follow your career growth. Your network is your net worth.
  • Find a mentor. Reach out to senior security people whose work you respect. Most are willing to do a 15-minute call with someone who's shown this level of initiative.

Q: "What advice would you give to someone starting a 90-day cybersecurity challenge?"
A: "Three things. First, commit to consistency over intensity — 1 hour every day beats 8 hours every Saturday. Third, don't get stuck on perfect understanding — if you don't fully understand a topic, move on and come back. Some concepts only click after you've seen them in multiple contexts. The 90-day challenge is about breadth first, depth second."
