CompTIA Security+ Exam Overview

Security+ is the most widely recognized entry-level certification. Today you understand the exam domains, question formats, and create a study plan.

THEORY

What is CompTIA Security+ (SY0-701)? Security+ is an entry-level cybersecurity certification that validates baseline security knowledge required for any cybersecurity role. It's accredited by ANSI and ISO, meets DoD 8570 requirements (the US Department of Defense baseline certification), and is the most common certification listed in SOC analyst job postings. Over 60% of cybersecurity job listings prefer or require Security+. The exam costs $404 (USD), has 90 minutes to answer up to 90 questions (multiple choice + PBQs), and requires a passing score of 750/900.

The 5 exam domains (SY0-701):

Domain 1: Attacks, Threats & Vulnerabilities
24% of exam — ~22 questions
Malware types, social engineering, application attacks (SQLi, XSS), network attacks, wireless attacks, cryptographic attacks, threat actors, attack frameworks (MITRE ATT&CK, Cyber Kill Chain).
Domain 2: Architecture & Design
21% of exam — ~19 questions
Defense-in-depth, secure network segmentation, virtualization and cloud security, embedded/IoT systems, secure facility design, fundamental security concepts (confidentiality, integrity, availability).
Domain 3: Implementation
25% of exam — ~22 questions
Secure protocols (TLS, SSH, IPsec), PKI/certificates, identity and access management (AAA, MFA, SSO), host/network security controls, mobile security, wireless security configurations.
Domain 4: Operations & Incident Response
16% of exam — ~14 questions
Incident response lifecycle, digital forensics, data sources for investigation (logs, PCAPs, netflow), mitigation techniques, disaster recovery and continuity, data sovereignty.
Domain 5: Governance, Risk & Compliance
14% of exam — ~13 questions
Security policies, risk assessment types (qualitative vs quantitative), business continuity, GDPR/HIPAA/FISMA compliance, vendor due diligence, security awareness training.

Question types you'll see:

Multiple Choice (MCQ): "Which of the following is the BEST control to prevent SQL injection?" A) WAF B) Input validation C) Parameterized queries D) Encryption Performance-Based Questions (PBQ): - Drag-and-drop: configure a secure network with correct firewall placement - Ordering: put incident response steps in the correct sequence - Matching: associate security controls with their types (preventive/detective/deterrent) - Simulation: configure a wireless router with WPA3 or set up a VPN tunnel PBQs appear FIRST — you can't go back to them once submitted. Answer PBQs, then go back to MCQs you flagged for review.
Exam strategy: Answer all PBQs first (they're weighted highest). Flag MCQs you're unsure of for review. Use the "eliminate wrong answers" technique — in Security+, two answers are usually obviously wrong, leaving a 50/50 choice. Trust your lab experience over memorization.
// attacker vs defender mindset
⚔ Attacker
  • The Security+ exam doesn't make you a pentester — but understanding the certification's attack coverage helps you identify what entry-level defenders are trained to look for.
  • Domain 1 covers the same attack techniques you'd study for offensive certs — SQLi, XSS, CSRF, buffer overflows, social engineering. Master Domain 1 for both Security+ and OSCP foundations.
  • Knowing what Security+ covers helps you understand what entry-level SOC analysts can detect — and therefore what they might miss.
🛡 Defender
  • Security+ is the baseline certification for DoD 8570 — without it, you can't work in many government and defense contractor SOC roles regardless of your skills.
  • The PBQ simulations test real-world configuration tasks — configuring firewall rules, setting up wireless security, implementing access controls. Practice these hands-on.
  • Security+ Domain 4 (Operations & IR) directly maps to your Phase 4 labs — IR phases, forensic procedures, and data sources. Your lab experience makes this domain easier.

INTERVIEW READY
Q: "Tell me about the CompTIA Security+ certification and why you're pursuing it."
A: "Security+ is the industry-standard entry-level cybersecurity certification covering attacks, architecture, implementation, operations, and governance. I'm pursuing it because it's the most commonly listed requirement in SOC analyst job postings — over 60% of roles list it. It validates my foundational security knowledge and it's a DoD 8570 requirement for government and defense sector roles. The exam domains align well with my hands-on lab experience in incident response, forensics, and risk assessment from my 90-day training program."
Q: "What's the difference between a qualitative and quantitative risk assessment?"
A: "Qualitative risk assessment uses descriptive scales like High/Medium/Low based on expert judgment and experience. It's faster and easier but subjective. Quantitative risk assessment uses numerical data — calculating SLE (Single Loss Expectancy), ARO (Annual Rate of Occurrence), and ALE (Annualized Loss Expectancy). For example, if a server costs $50,000 and has a 20% chance of failure per year, the ALE is $10,000. Quantitative is more precise but requires good data. Both are useful — qualitative for quick decisions, quantitative for budget justification."
Q: "What's the difference between a denial-of-service (DoS) and a distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attack?"
A: "A DoS attack comes from a single source — one computer sending excessive traffic. A DDoS attack comes from many sources simultaneously, typically a botnet of compromised devices. DDoS is harder to defend against because you can't just block a single IP — you need cloud-based mitigation services (like Cloudflare or AWS Shield) that can absorb traffic across multiple distributed scrubbing centers. DDoS attacks have grown to over 1 Tbps, far beyond what a single organization's internet connection can handle."
